Horry County, South Carolina Flood Zones

Map Description

This map consists of 6 Layers.

  • S_Fld_Haz_AR:Shows Proposed Flood Zones for Horry County.
  • Survey Cost Data Layer: We conducted a survey of houses in the county that have reported flooding. This layer includes pop up data that displays the number of times a particular location has reported flooding, the "out of pocket expense" reported, the "Non-Repaired Damages" reported, the reported "Household Income" and the names of the "Named Storms" that reportedly impacted each location.
  • FOIA w Flood Data Layer: This layer includes information from over 1,000 homes located throughout Horry County. The locations on this layer are color coded. The items that are marked with red dots report 1 flood event. The blue dots indicate 2 flooding events, and the purple dots indicate 3 flooding events. Additionally, this layer's popup diplays whether or not a house is in a flood zone, the names of the named storms that impacted that address and the name of the flood zone that address is located.
  • Horry County Precipitation Layer: Displays location of the weather stations located throughout Horry County. The popup gives in depth precipitation information reported from each station including the station name and id, the annual precipitation reported for a giving year, the max daily rainfall totals for a given year and the latitude and longitude of each weather station. You can select the forward and back button at the top of the popup to cycle through all the years that a particular weather station's data was available.
  • USA Soils Drainage Class: This layer classifies soils in seven classes based on the rate that water moves through the soil.
  • USA NLCD Land Cover: This layer displays a time series of land cover for the United States from the National Land Cover Database (NLCD), at various intervals from 2001 to 2016.

Flood Layers

  • 0.2 PCT ANNUAL CHANCE FLOOD HAZARD: Area of moderate flood hazard, usually the area between the limits of the 100yearand 500 year floodplains.
  • AE: Areas with a 1% annual chance of flooding and a 26% chance of flooding over the life of a 30 year mortgage. Base flood elevations (BFE) and flood hazard factors are determined.
  • X: Areas of minimal flood hazard, determined to be outside the 500 year flood plain.
  • VE: Coastal areas with a 1% or greater chance of flooding and an additional hazard associated with storm waves. These areas have a 26% chance of flooding over the life of a 30 year mortgage. Base flood elevations (BFE) derived from detailed analyses are shown at selected intervals within these zones.
  • A: Areas with a 1% annual chance of flooding and a 26% chance of flooding over the life of a 30 year mortgage. Base flood elevations(BFE) are not available forthese zones.
